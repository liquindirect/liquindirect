Introduction

Liquindo, (/lɪˈkwɪn.dəʊ) officially the Federal Republic of Liquindo, is envisioned as a crowdfunded island nation; a refuge from global instability, an experimental society blending modern innovation, traditional values, and self-sufficiency.

History

Liquindo would operate as a junta, led by Diego Liquin and below him his investors who would sway and appeal decisions. In a time pre-democratization, Liquindo would operate as an elective autocracy. The first dictator (Diego Liquin) would appoint 5 political advisors, after his death, these advisors would elect a new dictator, the new dictator would then appoint 5 new advisors allowing the cycle to repeat itself. The dictator's powers would be bound by his advisors, and since, he appointed them himself, there would be no political struggle unless the advisors truly doubt the dictator's ability to rule. Liquindo would be an autocratic regime ruled under the administration of Liquin's Plan. The director would serve as head of state. Instead of democracy, parties would be assimilated into the administration, under the approval of Liquin and those already accepted, and converted into factions, which the public can join This would avoid any severe political interference in contrast to the administration's ideologies.
